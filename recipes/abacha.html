Recipe

Description

Abacha is a local indigenous Igbo food, native to the Igbo ethnic group of Eastern Nigeria.

Abacha is very Nutritious and is served in traditional Occassions by the Igbos.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups Abacha
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1.5/2 cups Ugba
  • 1 cup palm oil
  • 1 teaspoon Edible Potash
  • 1 Whole Fresh fish
  • 1-2 Scotch Bonnet
  • 1 red onion
  • 4-6 Garden Eggs
  • 6-8 Utazi leaves
  • 2 Tablespoon Crayfish Powder
  • 5 Calabash Nutmeg Seeds
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Ogiri
  • Knorr Cubes and Salt to Taste

Steps in Preparation

  1. First, clean and gut your fish, then marinate with salt, curry, and bouillon cube or seasoning. Leave to marinate in your fridge or a cool place for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Then grill for 45 minutes on high, Soak the Abacha in cold water till soft, this should take roughly 10 minutes, and leave to drain in a colander.
  3. Rinse the Ugba with warm water to get rid of any dirt.
  4. Dissolve the potash in 1 cup of water.
  5. Blend the scotch bonnet, 1/2 of the onion, crayfish, and ground Ehuru till smooth and set aside.
  6. Roast the Calabash nutmeg, then peel and grind it in a mortar or mill.
  7. Pour the palm oil into a bowl or saucepan, gradually add the potash mixture. Mix thoroughly. You’ll notice the palm oil will begin to thicken and the color will turn yellowy. Be careful not to add too much and be careful not to add the residue at the bottom of the bowl./li>
  8. Now, add the pepper mix into the palm oil, fish, Ogiri, then the Ugba, stir and combine.
  9. Then add the Abacha, stir and combine. And it’s ready.
